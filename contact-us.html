Frequently Asked Questions

Taniti uses the Tanitian Dollar (TTD). Major credit cards are accepted at most hotels, restaurants, and shops, but it's a good idea to carry some cash for smaller vendors or in rural areas.

Yes, Wi-Fi is available in most hotels, cafes, and public areas on Taniti. While the speed is generally reliable for browsing and streaming, it might be slower in more remote areas of the island.

The official language of Taniti is Tanitian, but English is widely spoken, especially in tourist areas. You should have no problem communicating, but learning a few basic phrases in Tanitian can enhance your experience.

Taniti's tropical climate means you'll want to pack light, breathable clothing, swimwear, and comfortable shoes for walking or hiking. Don't forget sunscreen, insect repellent, and a reusable water bottle. If you plan to explore the rainforest, long sleeves and pants are advisable.

While the volcano is a major attraction, it is important to follow all safety guidelines provided during tours. Stay on marked trails, follow your guide’s instructions, and avoid areas marked as dangerous. It’s also advisable to wear sturdy hiking shoes and bring plenty of water.

Taniti has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s respectful to follow local customs. When visiting markets, it’s common to greet vendors politely and ask before taking photos. Bargaining is part of the experience but should be done respectfully.

Taniti is committed to preserving its natural beauty. Visitors are encouraged to follow “Leave No Trace” principles, such as disposing of trash properly, avoiding disturbance to wildlife, and staying on designated trails in natural areas.

Taniti offers a variety of traditional dishes that reflect its diverse culture. Be sure to try local specialties like Tanitian seafood stew, grilled tropical fruits, and traditional coconut-based desserts. Many restaurants on the island feature these dishes.
